Courses

Fundamentals of Emotionally Focused Therapy (EFT)

By ICEEFT | March 2025 | 6.25 hrs

I completed a foundational training in EFT, a therapeutic model based on Attachment Theory developed by Dr. Sue Johnson.

This course taught me how emotions shape our relationships, and how to support secure connections through empathy and presence.

Key insight
Healthy relationships thrive when we are Accessible, Responsive, and Emotionally Engaged (A.R.E.)

Though I’m still learning, this training inspired me to deepen my understanding of human connection—and to walk alongside others as a compassionate listener, not just a helper.

What Is the Difference Between Psychotherapy and Counseling?

October 2023

Participated in a professional seminar exploring the distinction between psychotherapy and counseling, with a focus on real-world application through a self-esteem case study. The session was led by academic experts including Dr. Titapa Chinkijkarn and included interactive discussion and psychological case analysis.

Psychological First Aid

This course didn’t just teach me the principles of psychological first aid—it deepened my understanding of what it means to truly “listen with heart.” I learned that deep listening isn’t about preparing a reply—it’s about really being present and understanding the other person.

One of the parts that impressed me most was about self-care and self-compassion. As helpers, we often focus so much on supporting others that we forget to ask ourselves: “Am I doing okay?” This topic reminded me how important it is to check in with myself, too.

I also appreciated learning about simple yet powerful tools like “Grounding” and the “Circle of Control.” They’re not only useful for supporting others, but also for helping me navigate my own daily life.
